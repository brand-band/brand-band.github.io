B R A N D   P L A Y
an interactive exploration of gender play in branding
S T A R T
Advertisements are contained within a larger idea of brands; products can be much more than just functional and utilitarian--they can also be symbolic. Many brands are built around a collective narrative, or stereotypes that we as a society make about the identity of the targeted audience.
N E X T
Let’s try a game to help us see this. Pretend you are a designer with a marketing firm. You were just given a contract by a headphones company to design an advertisement for them! The target audience is someone who:
- is an engineering student
- participates in Greek life
- wants to found a startup

I ' M  D O N E
How many times did you remake your advertisement? We can see that each individual’s reactions to your advertisement was based upon their individual gender identity and how they perceived your design. Many different factors can affect how they react, including their level of conformity and subversion with the stereotypes they associate with different characteristics you chose for your ad.

Perpetuating stereotypes through gendered branding doesn’t just affect individuals--it also affects and excludes entire communities. As you saw, this exclusion causes social and economic consequences.
N E X T
Think about it: were there assumptions or stereotypes that you were making about your target audience’s gender identity? Were there broader assumptions or stereotypes you were making?
C R E D I T S
N O T E
The opinions and biographies of individuals in this game are meant to be fictional. They were based upon our primary and secondary interviews, and we recognize they are not representative of the wide spectrum of gender diversity. With time, we hope to continue to collect accurate and diverse narratives.
